Our products

Natural Indigo Dried Leaves

Natural indigo dried leaves, derived from the indigo plant, are traditionally used for dyeing textiles a deep blue color. These leaves contain indican, which transforms into indigotin when fermented, producing the vibrant indigo dye treasured in various cultures.

Natural Indigo Leaves Powder

Natural indigo leaves powder, obtained from the indigo plant, is revered for its rich blue pigment. Used in traditional dyeing processes, this powder undergoes extraction and drying, retaining its potent coloring properties. It offers an eco-friendly dye.

Natural Henna Powder

Natural henna powder, sourced from the Lawsonia inermis plant, is renowned for its versatile uses. This fine green powder, derived from dried henna leaves, is celebrated for its staining properties, cherished in body art and hair coloring traditions worldwide.

Natural Indigo Blue Dye Cake

Crafted from indigofera tinctoria plant, the natural indigo blue dye cake embodies dyeing traditions. Compressed from indigo powder, it offers convenience, delivering rich blue shades in textile dyeing. Its eco-friendly nature aligns with sustainable practices.

Natural Indigo Blue Dye Powder

Derived from indigofera tinctoria, natural indigo blue dye powder symbolizes age-old dyeing methods. This powder, extracted from the plant, produces deep blue hues, maintaining eco-friendly practices in textile dyeing, also for indigo dyeing.

Natural Indigo Seeds

Natural indigo seeds, from the indigofera tinctoria plant, hold the promise of a vibrant dyeing tradition. Sown and nurtured, they yield leaves rich in indican, essential for creating indigo dye. These seeds support sustainable textile practices, ensuring a colorful legacy.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Indigofast begins its process with the careful cultivation of Indigofera tinctoria plants. These plants are grown in the rich soils of Tamil Nadu, ensuring the highest quality leaves. Once matured, the leaves are meticulously harvested by skilled workers, ensuring that only the best leaves are selected for processing.

Processing and Extraction

After harvesting, the indigo leaves undergo a natural fermentation process. This traditional method, pioneered by Mr. M. Tamilarasan, involves soaking the leaves in water and allowing them to ferment. The resulting liquid is then oxidized to extract the rich indigo pigment, which is the core of Indigofast’s product offerings.

Product Formation

In Indigofast, the next phase : The extracted indigo pigment is processed into Natural Indigo Blue Cake, Powder, and Leaves Powder. This involves drying, grinding, and packaging to preserve natural properties, ensuring the products meet high-quality standards for use in various applications.

Quality Assurance and Distribution

Each product batch undergoes strict quality testing to ensure purity and effectiveness. Once approved, they are distributed across India, including to local businesses and textile industries, ensuring timely delivery and customer satisfaction. Indigofast emphasizes customer support and satisfaction.

Our Process
Extraction of indigo dye from Indigofera Tinctoria

1. Harvesting the fresh natural indigo leaves (indigofera tinctoria)

2. Freshly harvested indigo leaves put into the tank directly, which is filled with water and soaked by pressing the wooden rods, for the fermentation process, over the night.

3. Next day once fermentation process is done in water and it turned blue, removing the indigo leaves from the tank

4. Realising the water to second tank

5. Pedalling the water, for the oxidation process and rest it over night

6. Water removes, and sedimented indigo paste collects

7. Boiling the paste

8. Again filters the paste, rest it over night to remove the excess moisture content

9. Paste is pressed and made it cake

10. Drying it in sunlight

11. Fresh indigo cake is ready and made it into fine powder

We don’t use any chemical in the entire process. This is a 100% natural product.
